    <h2>Object of the Game</h2>
    <p>
        To outmaneuver your opponent on a mysterious battlefield. The goal is to strategically reveal your army, capture enemy pieces, and end the game with a higher score based on your remaining forces.
    </p>

    <h2>Game Setup</h2>
    <ol>
        <li><b>The Board:</b> The game is played on a 4 row by 8 column grid (32 squares).</li>
        <li><b>The Pieces:</b> A full, 32-piece set of Chinese Chess pieces is used, including the custom <b>Archer</b>.</li>
        <li><b>Preparation:</b> All 32 pieces are shuffled and placed randomly on each of the 32 squares, <b>face-down</b>.</li>
    </ol>

    <h2>How to Play</h2>
    <h3>Starting the Game</h3>
    <p>Players decide who goes first (Player 1) by any random method.</p>
    
    <h3>The First Turn (Color Selection)</h3>
    <ol>
        <li>Player 1 begins by choosing any face-down piece and flipping it face-up.</li>
        <li>The color of this piece (Red or Black) is assigned to <b>Player 2</b>.</li>
        <li>Player 1 is assigned the opposite color. Player 1's turn is now over.</li>
        <li>Player 2 makes the first strategic move of the game.</li>
    </ol>
    
    <h3>A Regular Turn</h3>
    <p>On your turn, you must perform <b>one</b> of two possible actions:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><b>Flip:</b> Choose any face-down piece on the board and reveal it. This ends your turn.</li>
        <li><b>Move:</b> Choose one of your already revealed pieces and move it according to its rules.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Piece Movement & Capture</h2>
    <p>Pieces can only move to an empty square or to a square occupied by an opponent's <b>revealed</b> piece. Hidden pieces cannot be captured and block movement (except for the Cannon jump).</p>

    <h2>Ending the Game & Winning</h2>
    <p>The game ends immediately when the player whose turn it is has <b>no legal actions available</b>. This occurs when they cannot move any of their pieces AND there are no hidden pieces left to flip.</p>
    <p>As soon as the game ends, the scores of all pieces remaining on the board are tallied. The player with the higher score wins.</p>
